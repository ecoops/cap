Uncashed cheques from CRA

Find out if you have a missed payment or uncashed cheque you can claim from the CRA. Get a cheque replaced, if it was destroyed, lost or damaged.

If you have an old cheque, you can still cash it. CRA cheques never expire.

Look up and replace a lost or stolen cheque

If a cheque sent to you by the CRA was never cashed or a payment was returned to the CRA, follow these steps to confirm the uncashed cheque and submit Form 535 to get a new cheque issued to you.

You can find out if you have uncashed cheques and get the pre-filled 2 ways:

  • Online

    Using My Account or Register a Client, you can view any CRA uncashed cheque, regardless of whether it was a refund, benefit, or credit payment

  • By phone

    You must call both the Benefis and Individuals call centres to check for different types of uncashed cheques you might have on file, and get the forms mailed to you

Online

Find uncashed cheques on file with My Account or Represent a Client, download the pre-filled form and send it through "Submit a document" to claim your money.

  1. Where to find uncashed cheques

    To find uncashed cheques your online CRA account:

    1. Sign in to or register for My Account
    2. Select "Uncashed Cheques" on the My Account "Overview" page
    If you are accessing an account on behalf of someone else

    To find uncashed cheques in the account of someone you represent, refer to: Represent a Client

    To set up a representative, refer to: Authorize a representative

    To get access to tax records of someone who died, refer to Represent someone who died

  2. Get a pre-filled form from us

    To request a replacement for an uncashed cheque you do not have, you must get Form 535, Undertaking and Indemnity pre-filled with the cheque number and date payable for your claim.

    In "Uncashed Cheques" section, you can download and print Form 535, for each listed uncashed cheque in My Account or Represent a Client.

  3. Complete the form

    Once you have printed or received the pre-filled form from CRA:

    1. Print and fill out the remaining fields
    2. Sign and date the form
    3. Enter your name and address under the signature
    4. Have a non-family member witness, sign and date the form
    5. The witness must enter their name and address under the signature

    If the form is not filled out correctly, it will be returned to you.

  4. Send us the form

    To avoid delays in processing, send only one form for each uncashed cheque.

    Submit the completed form(s) through "Submit Documents" in My Account or Represent a Client.

  5. Receive your payment

    • Direct deposit

      The fastest way to receive your payment is by direct deposit.

      If you are registered for direct deposit, your payment will be deposited directly into your account.

      Sign up for direct deposit

    • By mail

      If you are not registered for direct deposit, a new cheque will be mailed to the address we have on file.

      Make sure your mailing address is up-to-date

    Existing debts with the CRA may be paid first

    If you owe an amount to the CRA, part or all of your payment may be applied to your debt. Any remaining amount will be issued to you as a new payment.

Replace a damaged cheque

CRA cheques never expire or become stale-dated. You can cash your CRA cheque at any financial institution in Canada. There is no charge to cash your CRA cheque.

If you are having issues cashing an old CRA cheque, mail the cheque to the Imaging and Receiver General Operations Directorate and ask to have the payment reissued with a current date:

Imaging and Receiver General Operations Directorate
Returned Cheques
PO Box 2000
Matane  QC G4W 4N5

Find uncashed cheques for someone who died

If you have a CRA cheque that was issued to a deceased individual, give the cheque to the person managing their tax affairs, or return the cheque to the Imaging and Receiver General Operations Directorate's mailing address.

If you are managing the tax affairs of a deceased person:

  • find out if they have any uncashed cheques on file
  • deposit the cheque into the estate account and complete their final tax return
