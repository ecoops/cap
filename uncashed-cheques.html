Receiving a payment from the CRA
Uncashed cheques and missing payments

Find out if you have a missing payment or a cheque from the CRA that you never cashed. Get a cheque replaced, if it was damaged, lost or stolen.

If you have an old cheque, you can still cash it. Government of Canada cheques never expire.

  • Look up and replace a lost or stolen cheque
  • Replace a damaged cheque
  • Missing direct deposit payments

Look up and replace a lost or stolen cheque

If you received a cheque from the CRA that you never cashed or a direct deposit payment that was returned to the CRA, follow these steps to confirm that you didn't receive the money and submit a form to get a replacement payment issued to you.

You can find out if you have uncashed cheques and get the pre-filled form 2 ways:

  • Online

    Using My Account or Register a Client, you can view most CRA uncashed cheques, regardless of whether they were for a refund, benefit, or credit payment

  • By phone

    You must call multiple phone numbers to check for different types of uncashed cheques you might have, and get the forms mailed to you

  2. Get a pre-filled form from us

    To request a replacement for an uncashed cheque you do not have, you must get the form (Form 535, Undertaking and Indemnity) pre-filled with the cheque number and date payable for your claim.

    In the "Uncashed Cheques" section of My Account or Represent a Client, you can download and print the pre-filled form for each uncashed cheque. If you are unable to download or print the form, you can also call us to request the pre-filled form by mail.

  3. Complete the form

    Once you have printed or received the pre-filled form from the CRA:

    1. Fill out the remaining fields
    2. Sign and date the form
    3. Enter your name and address under the signature
    4. Have a non-family member witness, sign and date the form
    5. Have the witness enter their name and address under the signature

    If the form is not filled out correctly, it will be returned to you.

  4. Send us the form

    • Online

      To avoid delays in processing, send one form for each uncashed cheque.

      Submit a scan of the completed form(s) through "Submit documents" in My Account or Represent a Client.

    • By mail

      To avoid delays in processing, send one form for each uncashed cheque.

      Mail the completed form to:

      Sudbury tax centre
      Post Office Box 20000, Station A
      Sudbury ON P3A 5C2

    How long it takes to process the form
    Refer to:

  5. Receive your payment

    • Direct deposit

      The fastest way to receive your payment is by direct deposit.

      Sign up for or update your direct deposit

      If you are registered for direct deposit, your payment will be deposited directly into your account.

    • By mail

      If you are not registered for direct deposit, a new cheque will be mailed to the address we have on file.

      Keep your address up to date

    If you have a government debt, it may be automatically paid first

    If you owe an amount to the CRA, part or all of your payment may be applied to your debt. Any remaining amount will be issued to you as a new payment.

    To see which payments will be used to offset which debts, refer to: How payments are applied to offset debt

    If you are cashing the cheque for someone who died, deposit the cheque into the estate account.

Never miss another payment

Replace a damaged cheque

CRA cheques never expire or become stale-dated. You can cash your CRA cheque at any financial institution in Canada. There is no charge to cash your CRA cheque.

If you are having issues cashing an old CRA cheque, mail the cheque to the Imaging and Receiver General Operations Directorate and ask to have the payment reissued with a current date:

Imaging and Receiver General Operations Directorate
Returned Cheques
PO Box 2000
Matane  QC G4W 4N5
