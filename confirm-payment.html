Payments to the CRA

Confirm a balance you owe or a payment you made

You can confirm that you have a balance due to the CRA. If you make a payment to the CRA, it may not be applied to your account right away. It will be applied when your payment is processed by the CRA.

Check your CRA account balance

You or your representative can sign in to your online account to confirm an amount you must pay or to see if a payment you sent has been received.

Before checking to see if the CRA has received a payment you sent, allow:

  • 3 business days for online payments
  • 10 business days (plus mailing time) for payments by cheque or money order

In My Account select "Accounts and payments".

In My Business Account select "View and pay account balance".

CRA sign in

If you cannot access an online account, you or your representative can contact us about your balance by phone.

Move business payments or balances between accounts

Businesses can transfer a payment online from one interim period to another or to pay an amount owing within a program account and between program accounts of the same nine-digit business number in My Business Account.

Find out more about how to move business payments between accounts

Resolve an issue with a payment

If you had a debt on your account, we may have automatically used your refund, benefit or credit to pay down the debt: How we automatically apply refunds and credits to your debt

If your payment was not applied to an account as expected, you or your representative can contact us.

Contact us about your individual tax or benefit account:

  • Canadian residents: 1-800-959-8281 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-8281
  • Non-residents
    • Canada or U.S.: 1-855-284-5946 (opens up phone application)1-855-284-5946
    • Outside Canada or U.S.: 1-613-940-8499 (opens up phone application)1-613-940-8499

Contact us about your business account:

  • Call 1-800-959-5525 (opens up phone application)1-800-959-5525

When calling the CRA to resolve payment issues, you or your representative will need to provide additional information, such as details from one of your assessed tax returns, to verify your identity and ensure confidentiality.
