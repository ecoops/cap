Pay at a Canada Post retail location: Payments to the CRA

You can make a payment with cash or debit card at any Canada Post retail outlet across Canada using a QR code.

A service fee will be charged.

How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of someone else, or for a business by visiting a Canada Post retail location.

Make your payment

You will need a quick response (QR) code that contains information that allows the CRA to credit your account.

The CRA includes QR codes on various individual and business remittance vouchers. If your remittance voucher does not have a QR code, you will need to generate one. Canada Post uses a third-party service provider to process the QR code. The QR code looks like this:

How to generate a QR code

You can create a personalized QR code for your payment by using My Account, My Business Account, or Pay Simply.

My Account or My Business Account
  1. Sign in to your CRA online account to generate a QR code.
  2. Select instructions to generate QR code--are they the same in MyAcc/MyBA?

    3. CRA sign in

Pay Simply
  1. Visit Pay Simply to generate a QR code.
  2. Select the tax type you want to pay, your social insurance number or business account number, your email, name, and the amount you want to pay.
  3. When you have completed the required fields, press "Continue". Choose "Pay in person with cash or debit at Canada Post".
  4. Confirm your details are correct. A service fee charged based on the amount of the payment displays in the summary.
  5. Select how you want to receive your QR code. You may choose to send to get the QR code by email, by text message or to print it at home. You can choose any or all of these options. If you choose receive your code by text message, you will need to enter your 10-digit mobile number. If you choose print at home, a print icon will display.

How to pay

  1. Bring the QR code on your phone or on paper to any Canada Post retail location.
    Find your nearest Canada Post location
  2. The clerk will scan your QR code and ask you how much you want to pay. The amount you initially entered when creating the QR code is not displayed to the clerk. The clerk does not see any CRA account information. The clerk will key in the amount you want to pay, add the service fee and accept payment by cash or debit card.
  3. The clerk will give you a paper receipt with the amount paid and reference number for your files.

How long the payment takes to be received and processed

  • Considered as paid: the same day it was paid (stamped by the clerk)
  • Updated in your CRA account: up to 2 business days after the payment is received. You can confirm if a payment was received.

If your payment still hasn't shown up after 2 business days, resolve a payment issue.
