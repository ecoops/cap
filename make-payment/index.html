Payment options: out of

  • Bank or credit union online

    Some financial institutions will allow you to set up the CRA as a payee to make a payment, or schedule multiple payments if you need to make a payment arrangement or pay in instalments.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawl from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: through a bank or credit union website or app

    • Received by CRA: Typically, 1 to 2 business days
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to 5 business days after the payment is received

  • Bank or credit union at the counter (teller)

    In Canada

    You can make a payment to the CRA at any financial institution in Canada.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawl from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: through a bank or credit union at the counter (teller)

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same day it was paid (stamped by the bank employee)
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

    Outside of Canada

    Non-residents may be able to wire transfer a payment.

    Payments can be made using a wire transfer.

    How to pay: through a foreign bank or credit union

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same or next (0 to 1) business days
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Bank or credit union at the ATM

    Before you go to the ATM: You may need to have the CRA set up as a payee.

    Payments are made through a direct withdrawl from your savings or chequing account.

    How to pay: at the branch ATM

    • Received by CRA: Typically, from 1 to 2 business days
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to 2 business days after the payment is received

  • CRA secure online services

    Single lump sum payment

    How to pay: by debit card through CRA online services

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same or next (0 to 1) business day
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

    How to pay: by scheduling a withdrawl (or series of withdrawls) from your chequing account through CRA online services

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same day as the schedule withdrawl (if the payment is scheduled at least 5 business days in advance).
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to 1 business day after the payment is received for it to be posted to your account

    Series of scheduled payments

    The schedule must be set up no less than 5 business days before the first payment is made through a direct withdrawl from your chequing account.

    How to pay: by scheduling a withdrawl (or series of withdrawls) from your chequing account through CRA online services

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same day as the schedule withdrawl (if the payment is scheduled at least 5 business days in advance)
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to 1 business day after the payment is received

  • Third party service providers

    Not every service provider will process every type of payment.

    Most service providers will accept a debit card, credit card, or online e-transfer (e.g. PayPal). Types of payments that are accepted will vary.

    Service fees will be charged by the provider.

    How to pay: through a third party service provider

    • Received by CRA: Varies based on the service provider
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Through the mail

    Stamped envelope

    You can send a payment with or without a paper return, from anywhere around the world.

    Accepted payments are a cheque or a money order.

    How to pay: through the mail with a stamped envelope

    • Received by CRA: Mail delivery varies, allow up to one week when mailed within Canada, or two weeks internationally
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received for it to be posted to your account

    Registered mail courier

    For extra security, you can send a payment with or without a paper return, through registered mail, from anywhere in the world.

    Service fees will be charged by the courrier.

    Accepted payments are a cheque or a money order.

    How to pay: through the mail with a registered courier

    • Received by CRA: Mail delivery varies based the service fees are paid to be mailed faster
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

    CRA drop box

    You can make a payment when you drop off a return or corespondence to a CRA drop box. No postage is required.

    Accepted payments are a cheque or a money order.

    How to pay: through a CRA drop box

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same or next (0 to 1) business days
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Canada Post retail location

    Before you pay at Canada Post: You must generate a customized QR code to take to the retail location in order to apply the payment correctly.

    Accepted payments are a debit card or cash.

    How to pay: at a Canada post retail location

    • Received by CRA: Typically, the same day it was paid (stamped by the Canada Post employee)
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

  • Offset a benefit overpayment

    A balance owing on a benefit may be able to be repaid by applying a future benefit payment.

    This payment method should only be applied to other benefit or credit debts.

    How to pay: by offsetting a benefit payment

    • Received by CRA: *need to confirm how long, refer to the benefits payment dates
    • Posted to your CRA account: It may take up to X business days after the payment is received

CRA does not accept certain payments

This includes but is not limited to:

  • Cryptocurrency

    We do not accept any form of cryptocurrency (e.g. Bitcoin) as payment

  • Gift cards

    We do not accept any gift cards in any form of payment

  • Travellers cheques

    We do not accept any travellers cheques

  • Cash by mail

    Cash can only be used at any Canada post retail location

      CRA's "My Payment" is not available to pay an ESDC debt

      2. Enter your Social Insurance Number (SIN) as the account number
      3. If your financial institution requires 11 characters, add the letters "YY" or the numbers "00" at the end on your entry

      1. Make your cheque or money order payable to "Receiver General for Canada"
      2. Write your client ID number on the front of your cheque or money order
      3. Include your remittance slip with your payment – do not staple
      4. Mail your payment to the payment office indicated on the back of your statement of account

      Do not send cash by mail.
