Pay through the mail: Payments to the CRA

You can make a payment to the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA) with a cheque from your Canadian bank account.

Service fees may be charged by Canada Post or courrier services.

On this page

  • How to pay the amount you owe

How to pay the amount you owe

Make a payment for yourself, on behalf of someone else, or for a business by sending a cheque to us by mail.

Include a remittance voucher

We will need the remittance voucher included in your envelope to apply your payment correctly to your tax account.

We accept photocopies of remittance vouchers if they are clearly legible.

If you do not have one, order or print a remittance voucher or a payment form.

Make out the cheque

Include the following details on the front memo field of your cheque:

Made out to
Receiver General for Canada
Memo field
  • Calendar year for which you are paying
  • Your relevant tax number
    • Individuals: SIN, ITN, or TTN
    • Corporations: business number and program account
    • Non-residents: withholding tax account number

Send us your cheque

Decide how you'll send us your mail:

  • Mail your cheque

    Address your cheque to:

    Canada Revenue Agency
    PO Box 3800 STN A
    Sudbury ON P3A 0C3

  • Drop your cheque at a CRA dropbox

    You can go to one of our locations to drop off your envelope yourself. Refer to: CRA office and drop box locations

Alternatively, consider paying in person at any Canada Post outlet using cash or debit card.

How long the payment takes to be received and processed

  • Considered as paid: the same or next business day (varies based on your bank or credit union)
  • Updated in your CRA account: up to 2 business days after the payment is received. You can confirm if a payment was received.

If your payment still hasn't shown up after 2 business days, resolve a payment issue.
