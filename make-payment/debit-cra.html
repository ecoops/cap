Pay with a debit card online with the CRA: Payments to the CRA

You can make payments directly to the CRA using your bank access card (Interac Debit, Visa Debit, or Debit Mastercard) through CRA's secure electronic service called My Payment.

On this page

  • What you can and can't pay
  • How to pay the amount you owe
  • How long the payment takes to be received and processed

What you can and can't pay

Payments we accept online through My Payment
Indivduals
  • Income tax (T1) amount owing
  • Income tax (T1) instalment payments
  • Advanced Canada Workers Benefit repayment
  • Canada Child Benefit (CCB) repayment
  • Canada Dental Benefit repayment
  • COVID-19 benefit repayment (CERB, etc.)
  • GST/HST credit repayment
  • One time top-up to Canada Housing Benefit repayment
  • Part IX GST Security deposit (non-resident)
  • Part XIII withholding tax (non-resident)
  • Universal Child Care Benefit repayment
Businesses
  • Corporation income tax (T2)
  • Corporation income tax (T2) instalment payments
  • GST/HST remittances
  • Source deductions (payroll)
  • Air Travellers Security Charge
  • COVID-19 business subsidy overpayments (CEWS, etc.) except rent subisdy
  • Excise duties
  • Excise tax
  • Excise tax on insurance premiums
  • Fuel charge
  • Luxury tax
  • Nova Scotia Workers' Compensation Board payments
  • Part XIII withholding tax (non-resident)
  • Softwood lumber products export charge
  • Underused housing tax
Trusts
  • Trust income tax (T3)
Payments we do not accept online through My Payment
Individuals
  • Alberta credits repayment
  • Climate Action Incentive (CAI) repayment
  • Ontario credits repayment
  • TFSA amoung owing
Businesses
  • Canada Emergency Rent Subsidy (CERS) repayment
  • Garnishment on behalf of an employee
  • Information Return (RZ), T5, RRSP, TFSA, etc. penalty
Charities
  • Re-register a charity

    21 hours a day, seven days a week.

    Have a secure connection

    Refer to: Access online services safely

    Use a modern broswer
    You will not be able to access My Payment using an older browser that does not support the latest security standards. Refer to: Your browser and troubleshooting tips

How long the payment takes to be received and processed

  • Considered as paid: Usually within 1 to 3 business days
  • Updated in your CRA account: Up to 2 business days after the payment is received. You can confirm if a payment was received.

If your payment still hasn't shown up after 2 business days, resolve a payment issue.
